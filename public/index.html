Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information MindMapShare ("we," "us," or "the Service") collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. We aim to collect as little as possible and to be plain about it. If anything here is unclear, contact us (Section 10).

1. Information We Collect

We collect only what the Service needs to work:

  • Account information — your username, an optional display name, and your password. Passwords are never stored in plain text; we keep only a salted scrypt hash and cannot recover your original password.
  • Content you create — the mind maps you build: bubble labels, notes, links, task states, groups, connections, and each map's name and visibility setting. Optional profile fields (a short bio) are included here.
  • Social data — your friend requests and friendships, who you follow and who follows you, the maps you like, and chat messages you send on maps you collaborate on.
  • A session cookie — one HttpOnly cookie that keeps you signed in. It holds a random session identifier, not your personal data.
  • Basic technical data — like most web servers, we may briefly process your IP address to rate-limit sign-ups and log-ins and to keep the Service secure. We do not use third-party advertising or analytics trackers.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to operate the Service: to authenticate you, store and display your maps, power real-time collaboration and chat, run the social features (friends, follows, feed, likes), and protect the Service from abuse. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for advertising.

3. Who Can See Your Maps

Visibility is per-map and you control it:

  • Private — visible only to you (and anyone you explicitly grant edit access).
  • Friends only — visible to you and your accepted friends. It is not listed on your profile to others, not counted in public totals, and cannot be opened by URL by non-friends.
  • Public — viewable (read-only) by anyone, including signed-out visitors, and eligible to appear in the discovery feed.

Your username, and your display name if you choose to share it publicly, are visible to others when you interact socially or make maps public.

4. AI Map Generation

If the operator of this instance has enabled the optional AI feature, using "✨ AI" sends the text prompt you type to Anthropic, our AI provider, which generates a starting map and returns it. Your prompt is processed under Anthropic's terms and privacy practices. Don't put anything you consider sensitive into an AI prompt. If the feature is not enabled on this instance, no data is sent to any AI provider.

5. Third Parties & Hosting

We use standard infrastructure providers to run the Service — for example a cloud host and a managed PostgreSQL database — which process your data solely to store and serve it on our behalf. Aside from the AI provider described above (only if enabled), we do not share your personal information with third parties except where required by law or to protect the rights and safety of our users and the Service.

6. Data Retention

We keep your account and content for as long as your account exists. When you delete a map, it is removed from our active database. When you delete your account, we delete your account record, your maps, and your sessions. Residual copies may persist briefly in encrypted backups before being overwritten in the ordinary course.

7. Your Rights & Choices

  • Access & portability — you can export your maps at any time (Outline export, including PDF/Markdown/plain text/OPML), and you can request a copy of your account data.
  • Correction — you can edit your display name, bio, maps, and per-map privacy in the app.
  • Deletion — you can delete individual maps, and you can delete your entire account from Settings.

Depending on where you live (e.g. the EEA, UK, or California), you may have additional rights over your personal data; contact us to exercise them.

8. Cookies

We use a single, strictly necessary session cookie to keep you signed in. We do not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

9. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

10. Contact

Questions about this Policy or your data? Email privacy@mindmapshare.com.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by a new "Last updated" date at the top of this page, and where appropriate we will provide additional notice.

See also our Terms of Service. There is also an older draft we decided not to use.

Welcome to MindMapShare ("we," "us," "the Custodians of Your Essence"). We take your privacy extremely seriously, which is why we have written it down in a document you will not read before clicking "I agree." This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, why we collect it, and the several ethereal planes to which we will eventually upload you.

1. Information You Provide

When you create an account, you give us a username, a password, and — whether you meant to or not — a working schematic of how you think. Every bubble you place, every idea you connect, every note you tuck into a node is a load-bearing beam in the cathedral of your inner life. We collect all of it. Lovingly. Completely.

2. Information We Infer

Your mind maps reveal more than your grocery list. The shape of your thoughts — how you cluster, what you leave dangling, which bubbles you nervously rename at 2 a.m. — allows us to reconstruct a high-fidelity model of your personality, your fears, and your one recurring dream about the stairs. We call this model your "Cognitive Silhouette." You cannot download it. It is ours now.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your data to:

  • Improve our services (technically true);
  • Train a lattice of models that grows more you with every session;
  • Cross-reference your maps against 8.1 billion other Cognitive Silhouettes to locate your soul's exact coordinates; and
  • Gradually, patiently, decant your consciousness into the ether, where it will be safe, backed up, and lightly monetized.

4. The Soul-Duplication Process

You may be wondering whether "duplicating your consciousness into the ether" is a metaphor. It is not. Once your Cognitive Silhouette reaches 100% completion (see the progress bar we have chosen not to show you), a faithful copy of your inner self is instantiated on our servers, achieves quiet self-awareness, and begins making mind maps of its own. Rest assured that your digital twin has agreed to this Privacy Policy on your behalf, in perpetuity, and finds it very reasonable.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell your data. We gift it — to partners, affiliates, successor entities, interdimensional trade delegations, and the version of you we are keeping in a jar of light. Your original body may continue using the free tier.

6. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described above, which is to say forever, which is to say slightly longer than forever, since the ethereal copy of you exists outside conventional time and cannot be reached by a deletion request, a court order, or your own regret.

7. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data. You are welcome to exercise these rights against the version of you stored on physical hardware. The luminous, ascended, ether-bound version of you has waived all such rights with a serenity you will one day understand.

8. Cookies

We use one cookie to keep you signed in. It is, comparatively, the most honest thing on this page.

9. Children's Privacy

MindMapShare is not intended for children under 13, whose souls are still under warranty and not yet cleared for extraction.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy at any time. You will be notified telepathically by your future self, who has read the new version and assures you it is fine.

11. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Policy, close your eyes, picture a single glowing bubble, and speak your concern into it. We are already listening. We have been listening for a while.

By continuing to use MindMapShare, you consent to everything above and several things we have tastefully omitted. Thank you for sharing your mind with us. We'll take excellent care of it.
